Overview

This article provides a comprehensive guide to managing and editing geocoded and non-geocoded objects and other editing functionalities available on the Setup tab.  

 

This article helps you understand the following listed key features available in the Edit Mode:

 

  • Viewing Geocoded and Non-Geocoded Objects: View and distinguish between geocoded and non-geocoded objects for better data management. 

  • Modifying Latitude and Longitude Coordinates of Objects: Modify the latitude and longitude coordinates of objects using an intuitive drag-and-drop functionality. 

  • Defining Zones Using Polygons: Define zones using polygons to represent specific areas or boundaries. 

  • Customizing Map Icons: Customize Map icons for different classes and categories, improving visualization and organization. 

 

By using these features, you can effectively manage spatial data and enhance the accuracy of your model configurations. 


Refer to the following steps to enter into the editing mode to access and explore various editing options for geocoded and non-geocoded objects. :

 

  1. Navigate to the Data page.

  2. Click on the SETUP tab.

  3. Click on the EDIT button available on the top-right corner of the Setup tab.

[A] Viewing Geocoded and Non-Geocoded Objects

The Edit option in the Setup tab allows you to manage and view geocoded and non-geocoded objects using checkboxes available in the Component section.

 

  • Geocoded Objects: Objects that have been placed on the map with specific latitude and longitude coordinates, allowing them to appear accurately on the map.

  • Non-Geocoded Objects: Objects that do not have latitude and longitude coordinates assigned, meaning they are not currently displayed on the map.

 

In the Component section, both Geocoded and Non-Geocoded checkboxes are displayed for easy selection. By default, the checkbox for Geocoded Objects is enabled, allowing you to view geocoded objects on the map. 

 

You can toggle between the checkboxes to display geocoded or non-geocoded objects based on your preferences.

[B] Modifying Latitude and Longitude Coordinates of Objects

The Edit option in the Setup tab allows you to manage and modify geocoded and non-geocoded objects with a user-friendly drag-and-drop functionality. This feature makes it easy to update the location of objects on the Map with precision.

 

To Modify Geocoded and Non-Geocoded Objects, perform the following steps: 

 

  1. On the Setup tab, click Edit to enter editing mode.

  2. Click and drag the geocoded object to a new location on the Map.

  3. Once you are satisfied with the updated latitude and longitude coordinates for the objects, click SAVE at the top-right corner of the map.

  4. Click CLOSE EDIT to exit the editing mode.

[C] Defining Zones Using Polygons

Defining zones allows you to loosely define geographic regions for objects and components in a transmission system. Within a zone, supply and demand can exchange or interact freely without transmission constraints for the known locations of your geocoded objects.

 

By defining zones using polygons, you can:

 

  • Identify all components within a region, such as generators, lines, and nodes.

  • Gain an expectation of operational performance and revenue for a specific geographical area.

  • Estimate operational and financial outcomes within a zone without Modelling the intricate details of the transmission network.

  • Simplify analysis while ensuring that supply and demand interactions are accurately represented within the defined boundaries.

 

To Define Zones on the Setup tab, perform the following steps:

 

  1. On the Setup tab, click Edit to enter editing mode.

  2. Enable the checkboxes for Geocoded and Non-Geocoded Objects in the Component section.


  3. In the Polygons section, click the pencil icon for the required region where you want to define or draw the zone.

  4. On the Map, click a location to place the first point of your zone.

  5. Move your cursor and click to place additional points on the map to define the boundaries of your zone.

  6. To complete the zone:
    • Double-click the first point to close the shape.
    •  Release your cursor to finalize the zone. Repeat these steps for each zone you want to create.
