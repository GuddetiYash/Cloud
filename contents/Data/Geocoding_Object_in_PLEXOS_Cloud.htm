Enable Latitude and Longitude in PLEXOS Desktop

You can enable latitude and longitude in PLEXOS desktop and modify these coordinates in PLEXOS Cloud. Geocoded data lets you see your objects on a map and visualize output providing new insights.

Geocoded data for objects can be exported for a PLEXOS Cloud study to a CSV file. PLEXOS Cloud lets you edit this data and upload changes. You can visualize and modify the coordinates for latitude and longitude within the map in Setup. These same locations are used on the map within Solutions. 
 

To demonstrate, the example in the  screens below shows PLEXOS desktop with generators listed on the left. 

1.  Click on one of the Electric > Generators > Attributes, and then click on Properties on the right upper panel.  As you can see, there are no Lat/Long columns defined. 

          

2.  To enable Latitude/Longitude, click on the Config option on the toolbar ribbon. You can see a class called Generator on the left. When you expand Generator and then Attributes, you see Latitude and Longitude checkboxes. The checkboxes are unchecked.  Enable the Latitude/Longitude by checking those boxes.

         

 
3.  Click on OK to save your changes.   

4.  Once the checkboxes are selected, you can see two new Latitude and Longitude columns under the Properties tab. 

       

5.  You can modify values directly in the columns and then click on Save. The modifications are saved to the model. 

     

Setting the Study Background in PLEXOS Cloud

The first time you view the Setup screen for a Study in PLEXOS Cloud, you will be prompted to set the study background. The study background is the default map view for a study.

  1. Go to Setup.
  2. When prompted to set the study background, hold Shift and then click and drag to select a region of the map.
  3. When you are satisfied with the map view, click Apply.  The map will open to the selected region each time you view the study on the map.


       

NOTE: You can toggle between Map view and Canvas view. Canvas view displays objects on a blank background so you can focus on memberships instead of geocoded map locations.
