Overview

The Energy Exemplar Marketplace is a centralized platform for hosting and managing a diverse range of Energy Exemplar products, including Playbooks, Solution Reports, BI Reports, and Tools. By consolidating these resources, the Marketplace streamlines the entire content management lifecycle - from creation and review to publishing and version control.


The PLEXOS Cloud Marketplace now allows you to create and publish Playbooks, Solution Reports, BI Reports, and Templates at the Private or Tenant level, transforming it into an Internal Marketplace for your organization. 

 

This functionality enhances collaboration by enabling teams to share, access, and refine shared content, fostering a more connected and efficient workflow within your Tenant.

 

➤  Key Benefits of creating and submitting content to the Marketplace:

  • Centralized Content Sharing: Establish an internal Marketplace where teams can create, publish, and access shared content, promoting consistency and collaboration across projects.

  • Seamless Collaboration:  Share Playbooks, Reports, and Templates at the Tenant level, making it easier for teams to work together and refine content over time.

  • Customize for Your Business Needs: Design Playbooks, Reports, and Templates tailored to your specific Modeling objectives, whether it's congestion analysis, emissions reduction, or cost optimization. 

Navigating to the Marketplace

Refer to the following steps to access the PLEXOS Cloud Marketplace.

 

  1. Sign In to PLEXOS Cloud using your credentials.

  2. Click the grid icon in the top-right corner of the PLEXOS Cloud interface to access the Marketplace.

  3. You will be redirected to the Marketplace page.

What's Covered in the Marketplace?

The Marketplace offers a diverse range of content to support your operational needs and it categorized in the following section:

 

  1. Global: Displays all publicly available content across tenants. You can select content from this section to Browse, License, or Subscribe as needed..  

  2. My Tenant: Shows content published specifically within your tenant. These items are visible only to users in your organization.

  3. Private: Lists content published at the Private level. Only the creator can view and manage this content.

  4. Subscriptions: Displays any Energy Exemplar's provided content you’ve purchased or subscribed to.

  5. Manage: Allows you to view and manage your submitted content. You can edit Playbooks, Reports, or other shared assets even after they’ve been published.

User Roles and Responsibilities

The Marketplace relies on defined user roles to ensure smooth operation and compliance. Users with Analyst and PLEXOS Cloud Analyst license types can create, submit, and publish Playbooks with the following role-based permissions:

 

 

 

  1. Content Publisher

    • Publishes content directly at the Tenant level without requiring additional review.

    • Content becomes instantly visible to all users within the tenant on the Marketplace.

  2. Content Moderator (Coming Soon) 

    • Acts as a Content Moderator for Tenant level, reviewing submissions from other users.

    • Can approve or reject submissions based on content accuracy and compliance.

    • Approved content is published and becomes accessible to all users within the tenant on the Marketplace.

[A] Assigning User Roles and Permissions

The roles mentioned above can be assigned by the Tenant Admin via the Central License Manager.

 

Refer to the following steps to add a new user with these roles: 

 

  1. Sign In to the Central License Manager (CLM) with your valid credentials.

    • Refer to the Central License Manager help article for URLs specific to your environment or region.

  2. Navigate to the Users page from the left navigation menu.

  3. Click ADD NEW USER, located in the upper-right corner of the screen.

  4. The Add Users panel will open on the right. Fill out the necessary fields.

  5. Select a License Type to either Analyst or PLEXOS Cloud Analyst from the Add License dropdown.

  6. Select the appropriate Marketplace role from the Add Roles dropdown (e.g., Content Publisher, User, etc.).

  7. Click Apply to confirm the role selection. 

  8. Click Save to add a new user. 

 
For step-by-step instructions on assigning these roles to the user, refer to the User Management page section under the Central License Manager help article.

[B] Content Uploading Limits

[I] Adding content in Playbooks 

A Playbook consists of Executive Views, Solutions, Maps Layouts, Grid Layouts, Solution Reports, and BI Reports. Below are the upload limits for each artifact within a single Playbook: 

 

  1. Executive Views: You can add up to 20 Executive Views to a Playbook. 

  2. Solutions: You can add up to 20 Solutions to a Playbook. 

  3. Map Layouts: You can add up to 20 Map Layouts to a Playbook. 

  4. Grid Layouts: You can add up to 20 Grid Layouts to a Playbook. 

  5. Solution Reports: You can add up to 20 Solution Reports to a Playbook. 

  6. BI Dashboards/ Reports: You can add up to 20 BI Reports to a Playbook. 


[II] File Upload Guidelines for Playbooks and Reports 

When enriching a Playbook or Report, you can upload images, release notes, and miscellaneous files. Below are the detailed file upload guidelines: 

 

  1. Images:  
    • Allowed file types: JPEG, PNG, GIF, SVG+XML, JPG 
    • Number of media files allowed: 20 
    • Max file size: 5 MB for each file   

  2. Release note: 
    • Allowed file type: PDF only 
    • Number of pdf files allowed: 1 
    • Max file size: 100 MB  

  3. Miscellaneous Files: 
    • Allowed file types:  CSV, XLS, XLSX 
    • Number of files allowed:  5 
    • Max file size: 100 MB for each file

[C] Managing Your Submissions

The Marketplace allows you to edit and manage your submissions, enabling you to make necessary updates to Playbooks or Reports even after they are published. You can access and manage your created content from the My Content section in the Marketplace.

 

Users with the Content Publisher and Content Moderator role can edit content visibility from the Private or Tenant levels.

 

Once the content is published, the following updates can be performed:

 

  • Edit Basic Details: Modify specific details such as the Title, Description, and Publisher Name.
  • Change Visibility Level: Adjust the visibility of your content, moving it to a broader audience.
  • Add License: Attach licenses to ensure only users with the specified license can access the content. 

 

Refer to the following steps to edit your submission:

 

  1. Navigate to the Marketplace.

  2. Click on your Profile Name located in the top-right corner of the screen.

  3. Click My Content.

  4. Locate the content that you wish to update.

  5. Click the Kebab Menu (three-dot-option) in the Actions column next to the content and click Edit.

  6. Edit the necessary details such as Title, description, and Publisher Name.

  7. Change the content visibility to Tenant if needed.

  8. If necessary, attach licenses to restrict access to licensed users and click APPLY. 


    NOTE: Adding licenses ensures that the selected content can only be viewed by users within the assigned licenses.

  9. Click SAVE.

Best Practices for Using the Marketplace

To maximize the benefits of the Energy Exemplar Marketplace, follow these best practices. These ensure efficient content management, secure data handling, and seamless collaboration across teams.

 

  1. Organize and Plan Submissions Strategically
    • Before creating or submitting content, ensure that all required information, such as Playbook details, Templates, and Tools, is ready and reviewed.
    • Use meaningful titles and descriptions to make content easily identifiable by others.
    • Align content categories and SKUs accurately to maintain consistency and clarity across submissions.

  2. Maintain Data Security and Compliance
    • For Privileged Content (e.g., Nodal Data), ensure CEII clearance before submission.
    • Assign appropriate licenses to sensitive content to restrict access to authorized users only.
    • Regularly review visibility settings and adjust them based on the intended audience (Private, Tenant, or Public).

  3. Leverage User Roles Effectively
    • Assign roles such as Submitter, Moderator, and Privileged Publisher carefully to ensure accountability and smooth workflows.
    • Use the Privileged Publisher role for trusted team members to bypass moderation steps when publishing high-priority or frequently updated content.

  4. Optimize Content Updates and Versioning
    • Use the Playbook Version Management feature to update existing content systematically while preserving historical records.
    • Clearly document changes in release notes when publishing new versions to maintain transparency and alignment.

  5. Enhance Collaboration and Feedback
    • Encourage users to utilize the Feedback Option on content cards to share insights and recommendations.
    • Regularly review shared feedback to improve content quality and address user needs effectively.

  6. Regularly Update and Maintain Content
    • Regularly review your submissions in the My Content section to ensure they remain relevant and up to date.
    • Update descriptions, visibility levels, or licenses as needed to adapt to organizational changes or project requirements.

Glossary

  1. A
    • Add to Cloud – A feature that allows users to copy content from the Marketplace to their Tenant for immediate use.
    • Analyst License – A PLEXOS Cloud license that provides access to various Modeling and analysis tools within the platform.

  2. B
    • BI Reports (Business Intelligence Reports) – Business Intelligence (BI) Reports are data-driven visualizations and analytical Reports that transform complex datasets into actionable insights. These reports help you interpret trends, monitor key performance indicators (KPIs), and make informed decisions based on structured and interactive data representations.

      BI Reports in PLEXOS Cloud provide customizable dashboards, charts, and tables that enable you to explore Simulation results, compare scenarios, and analyze system performance efficiently. 

  3. C
    • CEII (Critical Energy Infrastructure Information) – Sensitive data related to energy systems, requiring specific clearance for access. CEII-compliant users can view and copy privileged datasets.
    • Central License Manager (CLM) – The administrative tool used to manage user roles, licenses, and permissions in PLEXOS Cloud.
    • Content Publisher – A user role that allows publishing content directly at the Tenant level without requiring additional review.
    • Content Moderator – A user role responsible for reviewing submitted content at Tenant level for accuracy and compliance before publishing it to the Marketplace.
    • CSV (Comma-Separated Values) – A common file format used for storing tabular data in plain text.

  4. D
    • Dataset – A collection of data points organized for use in Modeling, Reporting, and analysis.

  5. E
    • Energy Exemplar Marketplace – A centralized platform for managing, hosting, and distributing PLEXOS-related content such as Playbooks, Reports, Templates, and Tools.
    • Executive View – A curated set of Business Intelligence (BI) reports that provide high-level summaries of key results. These views allow executives and decision-makers to review insights in a structured and concise manner.

  6. G
    • Grid Layouts – A customized tabular view that stores selected Properties, Objects, Filters, Pivot Views, and configurations for organizing Model results. It helps in structuring large datasets for better readability and focused analysis.

  7. K
    • Key Performance Indicator (KPI) – A measurable value that indicates how effectively a system or model meets its objectives.
    • Kebab Menu – A three-dot menu icon used to access additional actions, such as editing, publishing, or copying content.

  8. M
    • Map Layouts – A Map Layout represents a saved visualization of the Model's geographical data, including selected Properties, Objects, and any other selected parameters. It provides an interactive way to analyze spatial relationships within the system.
    • Miscellaneous Files – Additional files that can be uploaded to Playbooks, including .csv, .xls, .xlsx, .xlsb, and .xlsm formats.

  9. P
    • Playbook – A Playbook is a use case-driven dataset or Study that includes content to accelerate and simplify the Modeling experience. It includes pre-configured Models, Solutions, Solution Reports, BI Reports, and other supplementary content, providing a ready-to-use package for in-depth analysis and decision-making.
    • Playbook Version Management – A feature that allows users to create and manage multiple versions of Playbooks, ensuring proper version tracking and updates.
    • Privileged Content – Sensitive content such as Nodal Data that requires CEII clearance and is subject to strict access controls.
    • Public Content – Content published to the Marketplace that is accessible to all users.

  10. R
    • Release Notes – Documents providing an overview of updates, changes, and new features in a Playbook, Report, or Tool.
    • Report Version Management – A feature that enables users to manage multiple versions of Reports while preserving historical data.

  11. S
    • SKU (Stock Keeping Unit) – A unique identifier assigned to each content item in the Marketplace, used for version tracking and licensing.
    • Solution Reports – The Solution Reports are built to address common analysis scenarios and are useful as is or as a starting point to build your own Reports. These reports provide structured analysis with custom columns, totals, formulas, and calculations tailored to specific Modeling scenarios.
    • Solutions – Fully processed Simulation outputs that capture results from specific Modeling scenarios, allowing for deeper analysis.
    • Study Version – A specific iteration of a study that defines which Executive Views, Solutions, and Reports are available for selection in a Playbook.

  12. T
    • Tenant – A designated cloud workspace assigned to an organization, allowing internal users to share and manage content. It represents an organization within the environment.
    • Template – The Excel Add-in Templates provide seamless integration with the Marketplace, allowing direct data editing in Excel. Excel Add-in allows you to review and edit Model Input Data and synchronize changes to  PLEXOS Cloud directly from Excel. These templates enhance data consistency, accuracy, and efficiency for PLEXOS Cloud workflows.
    • Tools – Software utilities, including PLEXOS Desktop Installer and SDK Toolkits, that support energy Modeling and integration.

  13. V
    • Version Control – A system that tracks changes to Playbooks, Reports, and other content, allowing users to manage and publish multiple versions.
    • Visibility Status – A setting that determines who can access a published item in the Marketplace (e.g., Private, Tenant, or Public).

  14. Z
    • Zonal Datasets – General energy datasets that do not require CEII clearance and are accessible to all users with appropriate licensing.
