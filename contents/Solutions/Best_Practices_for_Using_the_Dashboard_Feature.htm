Best Practices for Using the Dashboard Feature

To maximize the efficiency and usability of the Dashboard feature, follow these best practices. These will help you organize your data effectively, streamline analysis, and improve collaboration. 

 

  1. Plan your Dashboard Structure Before Creation
     
    • Define the purpose of the Dashboard before adding widgets.
    • Identify which datasets and visualizations are needed for meaningful insights. 
    • Avoid adding too many widgets to maintain clarity and focus.

      ✅ Tip: Keep the layout simple and structured to avoid data overload.

 

  1. Use a Combination of Widget Types for Better Insights 

    • Utilize Grid Layouts for structured tabular data. 

    • Add Chart Layouts (Line, Bar, Area, Scatter) for trend analysis. 

    • Use Solution Reports for structured analytics and calculations. 

    • Incorporate BI Analytics Reports for interactive visualizations and KPIs.

      ✅ Tip: Mixing different visualization types ensures a well-rounded analysis.

 

  1. Organize and Standardize Naming Conventions 

    • Use descriptive and consistent names for Dashboards (e.g., "Monthly Energy Insights" instead of "Dashboard 1"). 

    • Standardize widget titles to reflect the data they represent. 

    • Keep labels concise but informative for easy navigation.

      ✅ Tip: A well-named Dashboard is easier to manage and share within a Tenant.

 

  1. Keep the Number of Widgets Manageable 

    • Limit the Dashboard to essential widgets (max 10 widgets per Dashboard). 

    • Prioritize high-impact data points for quick reference.

    • Avoid clutter by removing unnecessary or redundant widgets. 


      ✅ Tip: A focused Dashboard enhances readability and usability. 

 

  1. Export Dashboards for Backup and Reuse 

    • Use the Export feature to save Dashboards in JSON format for future use. 

    • Export Dashboards in PNG format for presentations or reporting. 

    • Keep a local copy of critical Dashboards before making major edits.


      ✅ Tip: Exporting JSON files allows quick restoration or migration to other environments.

 

  1. Avoid Overlapping or Duplicating the Dashboards 

    • Check existing Dashboards before creating new ones to avoid duplication. 

    • Merge related visualizations into a single Dashboard when possible. 

    • Delete or archive unused or duplicate Dashboards for better organization.


      ✅ Tip: Reducing redundancy helps maintain a clean and organized workspace.

       

  1. Utilize Shared Dashboards for Team Collaboration 

    • Encourage teams to share relevant Dashboards for better insight sharing. 

    • Use Shared Dashboards to align decision-making across different users. 

    • Ensure that shared Dashboards contain accurate and up-to-date information.


      ✅ Tip: Collaboration leads to more informed and efficient decision-making.

       

  1. Test Dashboards Before Publishing

    • Review the widget arrangement, accuracy, and dataset completeness. 

    • Check that all widgets are displaying expected results. 

    • Confirm that published Dashboards are accessible within the Tenant.

      ✅ Tip: Testing ensures reliability and accuracy in the final Dashboard.

 

By following these best practices, you can maximize the effectiveness of the Dashboard feature, ensure organized data analysis, and enhance collaboration within your Tenant.
